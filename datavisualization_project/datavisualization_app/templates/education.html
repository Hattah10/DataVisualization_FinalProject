Fig.5. The Adaptability Lvl Results of Bangladesh Students

During the implementation of online classes worldwide, in Bangladesh, most students who adapt to the new face of learning are grade schoolers [1 to 15], next are university students [21 to 30], and lastly are college students [16 to 25]. According to the results given, though making money was hard during the pandemic, the students were still able to adapt to the online class session; there are 51.9% of students - half of the respondents who were able to adapt to the rapid shipment of education, 39.8% who are having difficulty with the situation, and there are only 8.3% who were able to adapt the online learning comfortably.

Through this dataset, institutions can have valuable insights into how students can adapt to the new educational approach. As online education becomes increasingly prevalent, especially in the context of global events like the COVID-19 pandemic, understanding students' adaptability is crucial. The dataset can help educators and educational institutions understand how students adapt to online learning environments. This knowledge can be used to improve the design and delivery of online courses, ensuring they meet students' needs and preferences. By analyzing the adaptability levels, the dataset can reveal challenges that many students commonly face in online education. This information is valuable for addressing issues such as technological barriers, lack of resources, or difficulties in engaging with online content. All in all, understanding students' adaptability levels allows educators to tailor their teaching strategies to better suit the online learning environment. This may involve adopting different instructional methods, providing more interactive content, or incorporating technology in ways that enhance the learning experience.
